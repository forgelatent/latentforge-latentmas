Technique: Genomic Linkage Disequilibrium Encoding (Correlated-Block Pruning)
Field: Population Genomics
Core idea: In genomics, LD-pruning discards SNPs that are highly correlated with nearby markers, keeping only tag SNPs that act as proxies for entire haplotype blocks — transmitting one index instead of hundreds of correlated values.
Why LatentForge: Latent vectors from transformer models are notoriously block-correlated: attention head outputs within the same layer co-activate in clusters. Your top-k selection ignores this — you may be spending 30 of your 128 slots on dimensions that are near-redundant proxies for the same underlying feature. Apply LD-style linkage analysis offline: compute pairwise Pearson r² across latent dimensions over a corpus of delta vectors, cluster dimensions into haplotype-equivalent blocks (r² > 0.8 threshold), then elect one *tag dimension* per block. At runtime, transmit only tag dimensions; the receiver reconstructs the block using the pre-shared correlation table. This is injective within the learned block structure, directly serves your domain-adaptive goal, and the correlation table updates naturally as agents co-evolve — making it a living compression codec.
