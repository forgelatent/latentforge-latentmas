Technique: Sparse Distributed Representations (SDR) with Semantic Overlap Coding
Field: Computational Neuroscience (Numenta / HTM Theory)
Core idea: Encode latent deltas as fixed-sparsity binary vectors where semantic similarity is preserved as literal bit-overlap between representations, enabling O(1) similarity queries and union/intersection operations.
Why LatentForge: Numenta's Hierarchical Temporal Memory research shows that cortical columns maintain ~2% active neurons at any moment, yet semantic proximity between concepts is encoded as literal overlap in the active bit positions — not as cosine distance in dense space. For LatentForge, this means you could re-encode your top-k=128 delta vectors into a canonical SDR space where the shared seed vector defines the "null pattern," and deviations from it are transmitted as XOR masks against a known codebook. The critical advantage: two agents can detect whether their latent states are semantically aligned by counting shared active indices — no dot product, no decompression needed. The Shadow Self governance layer could use population overlap thresholds (e.g., >40 shared bits = aligned, <10 = divergence alert) as a biologically-grounded drift metric that operates directly on the compressed representation without full reconstruction. This sidesteps the current problem of needing to decompress before measuring divergence.

SUGGESTION 2
Technique: Genomic Differential Compression via Variant Call Format (VCF) Logic
Field: Genomics / Bioinformatics
Core idea: Transmit only the "mutations" from a shared reference genome (the seed vector), using position + allele encoding — identical to how sequencing pipelines store individual genomes as diffs against GRCh38.
Why LatentForge: The entire field of population genomics solved your exact problem in 2010: individual genomes are ~99.9% identical to a reference, so you never transmit the full genome — you transmit a VCF file containing only positions that differ and what they differ *to*. LatentForge's shared seed vector is structurally identical to a reference genome. A latent delta from Agent A should be encoded as `[(dimension_index, new_value)]` tuples only for coordinates that exceed a divergence threshold from the seed — this is a VCF record. The compression ratio scales with how "mutant" the agent's state actually is, meaning low-drift agents are almost free to communicate while high-divergence agents automatically produce larger packets — which is *semantically correct* and gives the Shadow Self a natural compression-size-as-health-metric without any additional instrumentation. You can further borrow the genomic concept of "phased haplotypes" to represent two coexisting latent hypotheses in a single compressed packet using interleaved allele fields.

Implement VCF-style encoding: store only indices where `|delta_i| > threshold`. Plot compression ratio vs. reconstruction fidelity vs. σ. Compare against your current top-k=128 fixed scheme. Expect VCF-style to outperform top-k at low-drift regimes because k adapts to actual signal. Pure Python + NumPy.

SUGGESTION 3
Technique: Fiber Bundle Compression with Structure-Preserving Projection
Field: Differential Geometry / Gauge Theory
Core idea: Factor the latent space into a base manifold (shared semantic structure) and fiber directions (agent-specific variation), transmitting only fiber coordinates after projecting out the base — analogous to gauge fixing in physics.
Why LatentForge: When two agents share a seed vector, their latent states live near the same point on a high-dimensional manifold. The difference between them has two components: motion *along* the manifold (semantic drift — important to transmit) and motion *perpendicular* to locally-probable directions (noise — wasteful to transmit). This is exactly the fiber bundle decomposition: the base space is the shared semantic manifold, fibers are the orthogonal complement at each point. Current top-k sparsity is geometry-blind — it treats all 128 dimensions as equally informative regardless of local manifold curvature. A fiber-aware compressor would first project the delta onto the tangent space of the seed manifold (using a locally-estimated PCA or Jacobian from a small probe set), transmit the tangential component with high fidelity, and either discard or coarsely quantize the normal component. This is how gauge theories eliminate redundant degrees of freedom in physics, and how modern neural geometry papers (e.g., Arvanitidis et al. on Riemannian VAEs) improve latent traversal quality. For LatentForge, the fiber structure gives you a principled answer to "which dimensions matter" that updates as the agents' shared context evolves — unlike a fixed top-k.
